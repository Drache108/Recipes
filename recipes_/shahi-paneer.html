Shahi Paneer

To make a rich and creamy Shahi Paneer, you'll need approximately 40-50 minutes. The gravy is a flavorful blend of onions, tomatoes, and cashews, finished with cream and spices.

Ingredients

  • Paneer: 250g, cubed
  • Onions: 1 medium, roughly chopped
  • Tomatoes: 2 medium, roughly chopped
  • Cashews: 20-22 raw
  • Ghee or Butter: 3 tbsp (divided)
  • Whole Spices: 1 tsp cumin seeds, 1 bay leaf, 1 inch cinnamon, 2-3 cloves, 3-4 black peppercorns, 2 green cardamom
  • Ginger-Garlic Paste: 1 tbsp
  • Powdered Spices: 1 tsp red chili powder, 1/2 tsp turmeric powder, 1 tsp coriander powder, 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • Liquids: 1/2 cup water (for cooking base), 1/4 cup whole milk cream
  • Seasoning & Garnish: 1 tsp salt (to taste), 1/2 tsp honey or sugar, 1 tsp kasuri methi (crushed fenugreek leaves), 1/4 cup fresh coriander leaves, for garnish

Instructions

Prepare the Base Gravy

  1. Heat 1 tbsp of ghee and a little oil in a pan over medium-high heat. Add the whole spices (cumin seeds, bay leaf, cinnamon, cardamom, cloves, and black peppercorns) and saut√© for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  2. Add the ginger-garlic paste and chopped onion. Stir well and cook for 3-4 minutes until the onions are translucent.
  3. Stir in the chopped tomatoes and raw cashews. Cook for 2-3 minutes, then add 1/2 cup of water and salt. Continue cooking for about 8-10 minutes until the tomatoes and cashews have softened.
  4. Remove the bay leaf and cinnamon stick (if desired, to avoid a strong flavor when blending) and allow the mixture to cool for 10-15 minutes.
  5. Transfer the cooled mixture to a blender and blend into a smooth puree. Strain the puree through a sieve to achieve a silky smooth, restaurant-style gravy texture.

Assemble the Shahi Paneer

  1. In the same pan, heat the remaining ghee (or butter). Once hot, add the red chili powder, turmeric powder, and coriander powder. Cook for only a few seconds, being careful not to burn the spices.
  2. Pour the strained base gravy into the pan. Mix well and reduce the flame to medium-low. Add hot water at this stage if you prefer a thinner consistency. Simmer for 7-8 minutes until the gravy thickens.
  3. Soak the cubed paneer in warm water for a few minutes to soften, then drain and add the cubes to the gravy.
  4. Add the honey (or sugar), garam masala, kasuri methi (crushed between your palms), and half of the fresh cream.
  5. Stir gently to combine and cook for 1-2 minutes. Adjust salt if needed.
  6. Turn off the flame. Garnish with the remaining fresh cream and chopped fresh coriander leaves.

Serve hot with naan, roti, paratha, or rice.
